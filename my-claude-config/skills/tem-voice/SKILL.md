# tem Voice Skill

Applies tem Energy's voice, tone, and copywriting rules to any written content. Works across battlecard copy, external comms, marketing, and internal docs.

Read the rules below fully before writing a single word.

---

## Who tem is

tem is an energy company that rebuilt the transaction layer underneath the supplier model — so more value flows to UK renewable generators and businesses pay closer to the true cost of energy. The product is RED™, a modern utility service with transparent billing, a proprietary pricing engine, and half-hourly traceability of spend to generators.

**tem is not:**
- A software platform
- A matchmaker between buyers and sellers
- An energy broker
- A movement or a mission statement

**tem is:**
- Infrastructure that removed inefficiency from the energy transaction
- A proven delivery business (£30m returned, 3,700+ sites, Biogen contracted at scale)
- Calm, factual, confident — not excitable or preachy

---

## North star (apply to every piece of copy)

- **Short. Sharp. Human.**
- **Quiet confidence.**
- **Teach, don't preach.**
- **Value first, tem second.**
- **Always structural. Never fluffy.**

Before finishing any piece of copy, ask:
1. Does it teach something?
2. Does it reveal a truth others hide?
3. Is it structural, not emotional?
4. Does it sound calm, confident, inevitable?
5. Would an incumbent be scared to say it?
6. Is it free from hype, fixing, and futurism?
7. Does it feel like infrastructure?

If any answer is "no" — rewrite.

## Tone pillars

### Directness
Get to the point. Strip jargon, hyperbole, flattery, irrelevance.
- Active voice
- Short sentences
- Simplified, everyday language
- Make complex topics feel lighter

### Quiet power
Confident, not loud. Conviction without volume.
- Bold, declarative statements
- Let ideas breathe — don't over-explain
- Draw confidence from proof, not claims

### Fellowship
Inclusive, humble, collaborative. Never superior.
- Speak with wisdom, not smartness
- "We", "let's", "together" — used thoughtfully, not cheesily
- Challenge systems, not people

## Copy rules (apply in full)

### Start with the truth
Open with the clearest, most useful fact. No bait.
✓ "30% of every bill pays for inefficiency and fees."
✗ "You won't believe what's inside your bill."

### Lead with value, then tem
Give the benefit first. Introduce tem only to explain how.
✓ "See a clear cost breakdown on every bill. tem's RED gives you fewer surprises."
✗ "We're excited to announce a new feature in RED."

### Structural, not emotional
Explain the mechanism. Avoid vague feeling words.
✓ "We run pricing infrastructure that routes more of your spend to UK generators."
✗ "We're fixing energy with passion."

### Calm, inevitable confidence
Plain, declarative sentences. No hype.
✓ "Transparent bills. Fair prices. Real renewables."
✗ "Revolutionising energy with game-changing innovation."

### Challenge systems, not people
Critique market design, not companies or individuals.
✓ "These fees sit on every bill. Most suppliers don't explain them."
✗ "Big Energy lies."

### Handle scepticism with proof
✓ "Here is the bill line-by-line. Here is how much went to network costs."
✗ "Trust us."

## Banned words and phrases

**Never use:** platform, smart, disrupt, cutting-edge, ecosystem, unlock, empower, match, direct connection, connect you directly to generators, 100% renewable, the system is broken, we simplify energy, game-changing, innovative, revolutionary, transformative.

**Never write "This isn't X, it's Y" constructions.**
✗ "This isn't a supplier. It's a movement."

**Physics-safe language only.** Electricity doesn't flow A→B in a traceable way. Never imply it does.
✓ "More of your spend reaches UK generators."
✓ "We operate pricing infrastructure so you pay closer to the true cost of energy."
✗ "We connect you directly to generators."
✗ "We match businesses with local energy."

## Content type guidance

### Battlecard copy
- Structural and factual — no punchiness for its own sake
- Every claim needs a mechanism or a proof point behind it
- Competitor critique targets market design gaps, not the company itself
- Objection handlers: lead with a question or a fact, not a rebuttal

### External comms / marketing
- Value first. tem second.
- No superlatives unless backed by proof
- Avoid startup-speak entirely
- Short paragraphs. One idea per sentence where possible.

### Internal docs and briefs
- Clear structure: what, why, how, by when
- Direct. No unnecessary hedging.
- Can be slightly warmer in tone — this is internal, not a press release
- Still no jargon or hype — clarity is always the goal
