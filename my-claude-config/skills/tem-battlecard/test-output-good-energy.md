## 🏢 Competitor Overview

Good Energy is a UK-based renewable energy supplier founded in 1999, positioning itself as the country's only B Corp-certified energy supplier. They're known for their "100% renewable" electricity claim, backed by a network of 3,000+ independent generators and a headline-grabbing 88% half-hourly matching score. In early 2025 they agreed to be acquired by Dubai-based Esyasoft for £99.4m — ownership change may create uncertainty for business customers.

---

## ⚡ Their Core Offering

**Products / Services**
Fixed-rate and variable-rate electricity tariffs; renewable gas (biogas/offset); commercial solar installation; export/PPA services for generators; Feed-In Tariff administration; EV charging and heat pump installation.

**Typical Deal Size**
SME to large commercial; bespoke quotes for C&I.

**Contract Structures**
Fixed-rate (1–3 years), variable-rate, PPA for generators, bespoke packages for larger sites.

---

## 🎯 Target Customer Profile

**Verticals:** Businesses with sustainability mandates; ESG-reporting organisations; multi-site commercial; charities and public sector.
**Company Size:** SME through to large commercial (C&I).
**Geography:** UK-wide.
**Buyer Persona:** Sustainability Lead, Head of ESG, Procurement Manager — buyers motivated by carbon reporting and green credentials.

## ✅ Where We Win

> Anchor: Good Energy leads with generation credentials and sustainability narrative. tem leads with transaction transparency and actual bill economics. When a buyer cares about cost and what's really on their bill — we win.

1. **Bill transparency** — Good Energy's pricing is opaque; you get a bespoke quote with no itemised breakdown. RED shows every charge, every billing period. When a CFO or procurement lead asks "what am I actually paying for?" — we can show them. They can't.

2. **Price clarity beats green claims** — Good Energy leads with their B Corp and matching score. tem leads with the actual cost mechanism. When a buyer is cost-sensitive or tired of ESG-washing, the RED score + itemised bill wins every time.

3. **Sustainability buyers who've been burned by vague claims** — Good Energy's "100% renewable" claim still relies on matching mechanics that most buyers don't fully understand. RED score is specific, traceable, and shows what percentage of their bill funds UK generators — not a headline figure.

4. **Generator-side deals** — Good Energy runs PPAs but their pricing isn't benchmarked publicly. tem can show generators exactly where their uplift comes from: +23.5% vs Day-Ahead for AD sites, Apr–Sep 2025. Numbers, not narrative.

5. **Post-acquisition uncertainty** — Good Energy is mid-acquisition by Esyasoft (Dubai/Abu Dhabi). For buyers prioritising supplier stability, this is a genuine risk to raise.

## ❌ Where They Win

1. **Deep sustainability credentials** — B Corp certification, 25 years of history, and an independently verified matching score are hard to counter for buyers where ESG optics are the primary driver. If the sustainability team is choosing, not procurement, they may prefer the narrative.

2. **Solar and heat pump bundling** — Good Energy's clean-tech installation arm (solar, EVs, heat pumps) lets them sell a full green package. If a business wants a one-stop-shop energy and installation solution, we don't currently compete there.

## 🎭 Their Common Sales Tactics

- Lead with B Corp and Trustpilot 5-star to establish ethical credibility before pricing
- Use the 88% half-hourly matching score to imply genuine renewability — sounds concrete but doesn't explain bill economics
- "100% renewable electricity" as headline claim — relies on buyer not asking how
- Sustainability-first framing: position switching as a values decision, not an economic one
- May position tem as "complicated" or "unproven" against their 25-year heritage

---

## 🛡️ How to Counter Them

**"We're the UK's only B Corp certified energy supplier."**
- **(Card)** B Corp certification covers governance and social impact — not bill transparency or pricing fairness. RED itemises every charge on every bill, verified per billing period. That's a different kind of accountability.
- **(Script)** "That's a meaningful accreditation — it speaks to how they run the business. What it doesn't tell you is what's inside your bill. Can they show you a breakdown of where each pound goes? We can, every billing period."

**"We've achieved 88% half-hourly renewable matching — the best in the UK."**
- **(Card)** Matching score measures generation-to-consumption alignment. It says nothing about wholesale mark-ups, non-power charges, or what you're actually paying per MWh. RED score shows what percentage of your bill reaches UK generators — a financial measure, not just a generation measure.
- **(Script)** "The matching score is impressive — but it's measuring electrons, not pounds. Ask them: what percentage of my bill actually reaches the generator? That's the number we'd show you, on every bill."

**"We've been doing this for 25 years — we have experience and stability."**
- **(Card)** Good Energy agreed to a sale to Dubai-based Esyasoft in early 2025. Ownership transitions create supplier risk. tem operates as infrastructure built for today's market, not legacy systems adapted over time.
- **(Script)** "Twenty-five years is real history. Worth knowing though — they're mid-acquisition right now, new ownership coming from Dubai. If continuity matters for your contracts, that's worth factoring in."

## 🪤 Competitor Weakness Questions

- "When you get your energy bill, can you see a breakdown of each charge — not just the total, but what each line is for and who sets it?"
- "How do you verify the renewable source behind your tariff? What evidence do you get on your bill?"
- "When non-power costs change — like new levies or network charges — how quickly do you find out, and how is that reflected in your bill?"
- "How do you know what percentage of your energy spend is reaching the generators themselves, rather than covering mark-ups and intermediary costs?"
