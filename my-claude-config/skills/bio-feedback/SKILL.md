# BIO Feedback Skill

The BIO model is TEM Energy's feedback framework. It stands for **Behaviour, Impact, Options** — a structure that works with how brains process feedback by grounding it in observable facts, connecting it to real outcomes, and giving the receiver choice over what to do next.

---

## The Three Elements

### 🔵 Behaviour — what did you observe, and when?
**Format**: "I noticed that..." / "I observed that..." / "During [specific situation], I saw that..."

- Concrete behaviour — not assumptions about intent or character
- Observable action (what they actually did or didn't do)
- Specific situation (meeting, project, time, place) — takes the receiver back to that moment
- Never: vague labels, mind-reading, or judgements about who they are

### 🟡 Impact — what was the effect?
**Format**: "The impact of this was..." / "This resulted in..." / "As a consequence..."

Impact can include effects on:
- pace, clarity, or decision-making quality
- team trust or psychological safety
- ownership or accountability signals
- customer, stakeholder, or business outcomes
- engine or project delivery

Keep impact grounded — don't exaggerate. Quantify where possible.

### 🟢 Options — what could they try?
**Format**: "You could..." / "One option might be..." / "Another approach could be..."

- 1–2 specific, actionable alternatives
- Choice-oriented language (empowering, not commanding)
- Future-focused — what they can do differently going forward
- Not every option needs to create more work; sometimes it's "keep doing this"
- For **positive feedback**, Options can simply reinforce: "keep doing this because..."
- Never frame Options as punishment or a task list

---

## The Most Important Rule: One Behaviour Per Chunk

**When the input contains more than one distinct behaviour, split into separate BIO chunks — one per behaviour.**

Do not blend multiple behaviours into a single BIO. Each distinct thing they did (well or poorly) deserves its own Behaviour → Impact → Options arc so each message can land clearly.
